The Books

Fiction About Non-Fiction

I am writing a six-book series. Four are complete. The premise: what if someone actually took the time to listen long enough to understand?

The fiction is the story. The non-fiction is the mathematics inside it. The characters encounter the framework — the three operations, the lattice, the sign swaps, the decode method — and the narrative is the experience of understanding it for the first time.

Because I have never been able to explain this to a human being in conversation. Not once in twenty-two years. Every attempt collapsed under the weight of its own dependencies. So I wrote the books instead — where the reader can take the journey at their own pace, in the company of characters who struggle with it the same way anyone would.
