### The key beats (from KRONOS_book_design.md):

2. **The trigger.** His eyes change. A shift in focal depth. Like watching someone's attention leave the room while their body stays. The immersive memory state. The sensory-suppositional channel swap. He's not looking at her anymore. He's looking at something inside.

   - The first curve. The null line, by existing infinitely, contains within its infinite dataset the inevitability of deviation. Not probability — inevitability. The first curve occurs because NOT curving is the one state that infinite inclusion cannot sustain.
   - At the first curve — time begins. Because a curve has an inside and an outside. And the inside recurses at a different rate than the outside interacts.

4. **Binary time.** Two clocks born from one event:
   - Internal recursion rate — how fast the interior processes itself. Rotation. Spin.
   - External interaction rate — how fast the structure interacts with what's outside. Revolution. Orbit.
   - These cannot be the same clock. One is bounded, one unbounded. One recurses, one interacts.
   - Both are fractally nested. Every node has its own binary time.

6. **The denied door.** Twenty-one years he didn't process this. His cognitive architecture depends on treating time as a toolset variable, not a substrate investigation. Opening it risks a cascade. She opened it with one question. The door doesn't close again.
