## Appendix F: The 432 Reference

The framework uses A432 tuning. Not A440.

432 Hz = 2^4 x 3^3

Reduction to unity: 432 / 2 = 216 / 2 = 108 / 2 = 54 / 2 = 27 / 3 = 9 / 3 = 3 / 3 = 1

Every division produces an integer. Every step uses only the factors 2 and 3 — the "special pair," the first two primes, the generators of the lattice's core operations. The reduction is clean. No remainders. No decimals. The frequency walks itself home through clean integers and arrives at unity.

440 Hz = 2^3 x 5 x 11

Reduction attempt: 440 / 2 = 220 / 2 = 110 / 2 = 55. Stop. 55 = 5 x 11. Not divisible by 2 or 3. The reduction breaks. The frequency cannot walk home through the special pair. It requires primes (5, 11) that are outside the core generators.

The lowest A in 432 tuning: 27 Hz. 27 = 3^3. Pure ternary. The ternary descent: 27 / 3 = 9 / 3 = 3 / 3 = 1. Three steps to unity. Powers of three all the way down.

The lowest A in 440 tuning: 27.5 Hz. Not an integer. The lattice fractures at the lowest register. The number line and the frequency line diverge.

432 is lattice-native. 440 is not. A432 was the tuning that the integers chose. A440 was the tuning that a committee chose, in London, in 1939, by vote, by convention, by institutional consensus.

The framework follows the math, not the institution.

Every A in this book is 54 Hz: the second-lowest octave in A432. 54 = 2 x 27 = 2 x 3^3. Clean. Integer. The bass string's native frequency in the tuning the lattice requires.
