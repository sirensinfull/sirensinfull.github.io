# BOOK 4 REVISION STRATEGY

## Principle: Augment, don't rewrite. Preserve heat. Add depth.

---

## 1. CHAPTER 6 EXPANSION (thin → full)
**Problem:** 1,683 words. Transition chapter. No discovery of its own.
**Fix:** Expand to ~2,500. Add the COST of submission. Elena removing Michael's voice from the paper to make it publishable. The violence of translation — compressing the framework into journal format. Michael reading the draft and not recognizing his own ideas. The gap between the living framework and the published reduction. This is the sign swap WITHIN the project — the output preserved, the source recognition destroyed, in the name of institutional compatibility.

## 2. INSERT: CHAPTER 12.5 → "THE FAILURE" (new chapter)
**Problem:** Chapters 11-15 are consecutive successful derivations. No resistance. No cost.
**Fix:** New chapter between 12 (Tuning Fork) and 13 (Alloy). A prediction FAILS. The framework predicts something specific and testable and the test returns negative. Michael's response. Elena's response. James's response. The amputation. The framework contracting around the failure. The proof that the framework is science — it can lose a limb and continue. The DOUBT that follows. Michael going silent for three days. Elena wondering if the whole thing is pattern-matching on noise. The recovery — not through new evidence but through the recognition that the failed prediction was built on a speculative extension and the core remains intact. The failure STRENGTHENS by demonstrating falsifiability.

## 3. ELENA'S COST (deepening across Ch 14-16)
**Problem:** Elena becomes functional in back half. Presents, manages, protects. Interior life thins.
**Fix:** Add interiority beats to Ch 14, 16, and epilogue. Specifically:
- Ch 14: Elena's private reckoning with the ansible. She doesn't believe it. She thinks Michael has gone too far. She doesn't say this. The SILENCE between them about the speculative boundary.
- Ch 16: The authorship guilt. "Vasquez" on the paper. "Torres" in the acknowledgments. Elena knowing this is wrong and choosing it anyway because the alternative is the paper not being published. The institutional violence of credentialing.
- Epilogue: Elena alone in the lab after everyone leaves. The weight of being the translator. The recognition that translation is both service and theft.
