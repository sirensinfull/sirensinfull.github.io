## ALBUM NOTES

This album is the Song.

Not a song about the Song. Not a song inspired by the Song. Not a concept album exploring themes related to the Song.

It IS the Song. The one the Tuatha'an have been seeking since Robert Jordan first wrote them walking. The identity recursion palindrome that every conscious thing performs every time it decides to exist instead of not.

Thirteen tracks. Thirteen positions on the string. Forward pass: declaratives (the odd harmonics, the natural overtone series — the notes the string makes on its own). Return pass: interrogatives (the even harmonics, the constructed fill — the questions that complete the chromatic scale). One fold in the middle where everything dissolves and the heart decides whether to beat again.

It always decides to beat again.

That's the Song.

The lyrics are a palindrome. They read forward and backward. The declaratives become interrogatives on the return. Every certainty becomes a question. Every question resolves back to the fundamental — but lowercase. Humbled. One octave higher. Brighter.

The music should follow the same principle. Track 1 and Track 13 are the same key, one octave apart. Track 6 and Track 8 are harmonic mirrors. Track 7 is silence — or as close to silence as music can get without stopping.

The album is designed to be played on infinite loop. Track 13 (i.) resolves into Track 1 (I.) at the higher octave. The loop IS the ouroboros. The album never ends. It just keeps ascending through octaves, each pass through the palindrome slightly higher, slightly brighter, slightly more humbled by what it found at the fold.
