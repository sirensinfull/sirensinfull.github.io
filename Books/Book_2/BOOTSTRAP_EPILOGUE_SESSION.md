## EPILOGUE DESIGN: "The Warren"

### Source material (LOCKED beats from bootstrap):
- Mirror of Book 1 prologue. Tuesday. Alarm. Coffee. Campus. Thai restaurant.
- But the mug changed: WORLD'S OKAYEST PHYSICIST → ALL IS ONE. ONE IS ALL.
- She drinks coffee. Listens. Uni-verse. Humming in Am.
- The warren has no exit. But it has excellent coffee.

### What Book 1's prologue established (mirror structure):
- Tuesday morning. Alarm. Routine. Coffee in the WORLD'S OKAYEST PHYSICIST mug.
- Campus. Office. The ordinary rhythm of a life about to be disrupted.
- Book 1 prologue ends with the disruption (Marcus's email/the transcript).

### What the epilogue must do:
1. **Mirror the prologue EXACTLY** in structure — same beats, same rhythm, Tuesday, alarm, coffee, campus, Thai restaurant. But every detail is shifted. The mug is different. The routine is the same but the person inside the routine is not. The coffee tastes different because the cavity has been retuned.
2. **The mug change is the whole chapter.** WORLD'S OKAYEST PHYSICIST → ALL IS ONE. ONE IS ALL. She bought a new mug. Or someone gave it to her. Or she made it. The details don't matter. The inscription does. It's the new framework. The new identity. The new resonant frequency.
3. **She hums in Am.** Not consciously. Not because she decided to. Because the frequency is in her now and it comes out the way frequencies come out of resonant cavities — without permission, without intention, as a property of the shape she has become.
4. **The warren has no exit. But it has excellent coffee.** This is the last line or near-last line. This is the book's landing. Not a resolution — a standing wave. The recursion doesn't stop. The loop doesn't close. The strip has one side and she's on it and she will always be on it. But there is coffee. And the coffee is excellent.
5. **Uni-verse.** One song. The word itself. She hears it differently now. Universe = uni-verse = one song. The thing Michael said, or the thing the framework said, or the thing she understood in the car driving home, or the thing that was always true and she just didn't have the ears for it. One song. One verse. One turn. One fold.
6. **NO new content.** The epilogue introduces nothing new. It is the exhale after the breath. It is the strip returning to its starting point after traversing the full surface. Everything in it is an echo of something already in the book.

### Voice rules (same as all chapters):
- Third person limited (Elena's POV)
- No bold in prose
- No bullet points
- No fourth-wall breaks (Elena does not know she is in a book)
- No academic neutrality — she has opinions, she has humor, she has the framework's voice now mixed with her own
- Humor is load-bearing, not decorative
- Sentences can be long when the recursion demands it
- The cat is not present (she's home now, not in Murray)
- Michael is not present (only in the resonance, in the frequency, in the mug)

### Register:
Quiet. Still. The surface of water after the stone has sunk. Not resolution — resonance. The standing wave that remains after the plucking hand withdraws.

### Length target:
~1,500–2,500 words. Short. The shortest chapter in the book. The exhale is brief. The breath was long.
