# Chapter 4: The Void Winks

---

## I.

It started with Bleach.

Not the chemical. The anime. Michael was on the couch — not the kitchen table, the COUCH, the couch that was the other command center, the command center for the hours when the body demanded horizontality and the mind demanded input and the compromise was a screen playing stories while the parallel architecture processed in the background. The couch was where Michael "wasted time." The couch was where the lattice digested.

Elena was on the other end of the couch. She had been on the other end of the couch for three episodes. She had been taking notes for the first episode. She had stopped taking notes during the second episode. She was now watching the third episode with the legal pad closed and the pen capped because the thing Michael was showing her was not a thing that could be tagged.

"Watch Ichigo," Michael said. "Not the fight. The PROCESS."

Elena watched Ichigo. On screen, the orange-haired protagonist was sitting cross-legged in an inner world — a sideways city, buildings protruding horizontally, gravity rotated ninety degrees. Across from him sat his zanpakutō spirit. His sword's soul. The anthropomorphized manifestation of his own power, externalized as a separate entity that he could see and hear and speak with and argue with and fight and lose to and learn from.

"That," Michael said. "THAT. That is the process."

"What process?"

"The process of building a direct line of communication with the psychological substrate."
