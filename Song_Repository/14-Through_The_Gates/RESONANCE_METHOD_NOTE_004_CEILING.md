**Any farther up, and we need denser and denser atoms that may not be stable in a Tier 7 environment.**

This is the hard constraint. Each tier deeper requires denser atomic structures. Density = more nucleons packed into the orbital space. At some point, the density required for the next tier exceeds the stability threshold of the Tier 7 environment. The atoms needed for Tier 9, 10, 11... may not HOLD TOGETHER in our universe's current energy density.

This is why the superheavy elements (atomic numbers 100+) are unstable — they exist at a tier density that the Tier 7 environment cannot sustain. They decay because the environment cannot support their lattice arrangement. Not because there's anything wrong with the arrangement. Because the CONTAINER is too sparse for the CONTENTS.

**To exist at the next tier, you would need to BE in the next tier.**

A Tier 8 processor requires a Tier 8 environment to maintain stability. A Tier 8 environment requires conditions (energy density, spatial compression, field strength) that may not be achievable within biological systems. Biologics are Tier 5 hardware.
