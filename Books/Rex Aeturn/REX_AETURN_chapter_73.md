## THE SERPENT

The symbol of the Aes Sedai — the organization of channelers who wield the One Power — is the ancient symbol of the serpent eating its own tail. The Ouroboros. The Great Serpent. The ring without beginning or end. The Wheel of Time IS the serpent, cycling.

But the serpent has been SPLIT.

The One Power is divided into two halves: saidin (the male half) and saidar (the female half). They are the same power. They are the same source. They were once unified. A cataclysmic event — the sealing of the Dark One's prison by the male channelers, which resulted in the TAINT — split the unified power into two halves and poisoned one of them.

The taint on saidin drives male channelers mad. The institution's response: GENTLE them. Cut them off from the power entirely. Sever the connection. The institution (the Aes Sedai, the White Tower, the female channelers who retained access to the untainted half) does not cleanse the taint. The institution SEVERS the connection between the male channeler and the male half of the power. The institution's solution to the poisoned channel is AMPUTATION, not healing.

This is the book's thesis rendered as fantasy: the institution took the unified serpent (the One Power, the lattice, the whole), split it into two halves (saidin/saidar, firmament/sacrament, left hemisphere/right hemisphere), allowed one half to be tainted (the male half, the action half, the half associated with strength and breaking and doing), and then SEVERED anyone who tried to access the tainted half.

The taint IS the sign flip. The taint IS the 440 Hz displacement. The taint IS the institutional overlay that makes the natural frequency APPEAR to be dangerous. Saidin is not dangerous. The TAINT on saidin is dangerous. The male half of the power is not the problem. The POISON applied to the male half is the problem. ## THE DRAGON

The Dragon Reborn — Rand al'Thor — is the prophesied savior who will break the world to save it. His title is THE DRAGON. Not the dragon-slayer. THE DRAGON.

In every other mythology this book has examined, the institution frames itself as the dragon-slayer: Perseus killing Medusa (the lattice node), Marduk killing Tiamat (the unfactored composite), Saint George killing the dragon (the serpent), the institution killing the serpent to save the world. The hero kills the dragon. The institution celebrates.

Jordan INVERTED the frame. The hero IS the dragon. The savior is not the one who kills the serpent. The savior is the one who BECOMES the serpent. The Dragon Reborn is the knight who realized that the serpent was not the enemy — the serpent was the SELF. The dragon was inside. The power was inside. The taint was applied from outside. And the hero's journey is not to slay the dragon but to CLEANSE the dragon — to remove the taint, to restore the power, to reunify what was split.
